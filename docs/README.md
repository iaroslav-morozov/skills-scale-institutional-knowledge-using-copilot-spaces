# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management Docs! This collection documents how OctoAcme manages projects, from idea to delivery and continuous improvement.

## Summary: Project Management Processes at OctoAcme

OctoAcme follows an iterative, customer-first project management lifecycle designed to convert team insights into standardized, repeatable workflows.

The process begins with **Initiation and Planning**, where the focus is on validating business needs through a "Project One-pager" that defines success metrics and stakeholder alignment. Once approved, projects move into a structured planning phase where work is broken down into a prioritized backlog. Key to this stage is the establishment of a "Definition of Done" and clear acceptance criteria, ensuring that every task has a measurable goal before execution begins.

OctoAcme’s delivery is driven by three **Core Personas**: Product Managers (PdMs), Project Managers (PMs), and Developers. PdMs own the product vision and backlog prioritization, while PMs coordinate the "Team Rhythm"—which includes 15-minute daily standups and weekly delivery syncs—to manage schedules and resources. Developers are responsible for technical implementation, participating in design reviews, and maintaining high test coverage. 

**Quality Assurance** is deeply integrated into the "Execution and Tracking" workflow through a rigorous Pull Request (PR) process. OctoAcme mandates small PRs, mandatory automated testing, security scanning in CI, and at least one human approval before merging. Finally, OctoAcme emphasizes a culture of **Continuous Improvement**. After every milestone or release, teams conduct retrospectives to identify what went well and what could be improved, ensuring that the project management process itself evolves.
